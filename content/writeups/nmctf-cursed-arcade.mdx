> **Category:** OSINT | **Flag:** `nmctf{Westwood_College}`

The premise I gave players: *two people died playing the same game, years apart. Conspiracy theorist **IntruderAlert82** believed a cult was behind it — now he's gone too. Find the place they used to operate from.*

## step 1 — find the theorist

The username is the thread to pull: `@IntruderAlert82` surfaces a Twitter/X account ("Truth seeker") with a four-tweet thread. Decode them in order.

## step 2 — the cursed game

> *"They expect us to believe it was natural? A healthy 18-year-old's heart just stops dead..."*

An 18-year-old dying after an arcade session points straight at **Berzerk** — in 1982, Peter Burkowski collapsed after posting a high score. The handle confirms it: *"Intruder alert!"* is the game's famous voice line. The '82 is baked into his username.

## step 3 — geo-lock the town

> *"They built it right next to where the dead sleep. That's not an accident. That's a message."*

Peter died at Friar Tuck's Game Room in **Calumet City, Illinois** — a town with the massive Holy Cross Cemetery sitting right in the middle of it. Geographically locked.

## step 4 — Otto is watching

> *"Every time I drive between the sites... Otto is always there. Watching me."*

Evil Otto is Berzerk's bouncing yellow smiley villain. A smiley face you can *drive past* in Calumet City? The famous **smiley-face water towers** — either search `Calumet City "smiley face"` or ride between the sites in Street View and they stare right at you.

## step 5 — the note in the reviews

> *"I left a note there in case anything happens to me."*

A digital note at a physical landmark lives in one place: **Google Maps reviews**. On the water towers' listing, a review from "Truth seeker":

> *"Whatever they were running out of this spot, they didn't just leave. They vanished. Scrubbed everything. Like it never existed."*

## step 6 — Street View time travel

"Scrubbed... like it never existed" means the present is lying. Drop the pegman at the towers, hit the **clock icon** for historical imagery, and drag back to **2011**: a huge building that no longer exists stands beneath the towers — **Westwood College**, a controversial for-profit institution that shut down after predatory-practice lawsuits. Very cult-like indeed.

Flag: `nmctf{Westwood_College}`

## design notes (from the author)

- Every step is a different OSINT muscle: social recon → historical research → geolocation → review mining → archival imagery.
- The real 1982 Berzerk death grounds the fiction — players learn an actual piece of weird history on the way.
- The Street View step is the boss fight: most players stall until they realize Google keeps a time machine in plain sight.
